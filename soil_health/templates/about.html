About the Soil Health Assessment Tool

What is the Soil Health Assessment Tool?

The Soil Health Assessment Tool is a machine learning-based application designed to help farmers, gardeners, and agricultural professionals assess the health of their soil and make informed decisions about crop selection and soil management.

How it Works

The tool uses machine learning models trained on soil data to predict:

  • Soil Fertility Level - Categorized as High, Medium, or Low
  • Soil Quality Score - A numerical score from 0 to 100
  • Suitable Crops - A list of crops that would grow well in the given soil conditions
Input Parameters

The tool requires the following soil parameters:

Parameter Unit Range Description
Nitrogen (N) ppm 0-100 Essential for leaf and vegetative growth
Phosphorus (P) ppm 0-100 Important for root development and flowering
Potassium (K) ppm 0-100 Helps in overall plant health and disease resistance
pH - 4.0-8.5 Measure of soil acidity or alkalinity
Electrical Conductivity (EC) dS/m 0-4 Indicates salt content in soil
Moisture Content % 0-100 Amount of water present in the soil
Organic Matter % 0-10 Decomposed plant and animal material in soil
The Machine Learning Model

The tool uses Random Forest models trained on a comprehensive dataset of soil samples. The models have been optimized to provide accurate predictions for a wide range of soil conditions.

Interpreting the Results
  • Fertility Level: Indicates the overall fertility of the soil.
  • Quality Score: A numerical representation of soil health, with higher scores indicating healthier soil.
  • Suitable Crops: A list of crops that are likely to thrive in the given soil conditions.
Improving Soil Health

If your soil health assessment indicates poor soil quality, consider the following improvements:

  • Add organic matter (compost, manure) to improve soil structure and nutrient content
  • Adjust pH using lime (to raise pH) or sulfur (to lower pH)
  • Add appropriate fertilizers based on nutrient deficiencies
  • Implement crop rotation to prevent nutrient depletion
  • Use cover crops to prevent erosion and add organic matter
  • Improve drainage for waterlogged soils
